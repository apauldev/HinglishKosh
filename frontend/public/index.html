Why HinglishKosh?

Over 600 million people in India communicate daily in Hinglish — a natural blend of Hindi and English written in the Roman script. It's how you text your friends, type on WhatsApp, and scroll through social media.

Yet before this, there was no open dictionary for it.

Academic resources use ISO 15919 transliteration (cāy, pānī) — accurate but nobody types that way. Commercial keyboards keep their dictionaries closed. So when your phone suggests the wrong spelling as you type, you're out of luck.

HinglishKosh fixes that.

209,462 entries

Sourced from Hindi WordNet (IIT Bombay) and Wiktionary. Every entry has a Devanagari headword, informal romanization, and English definition.

Informal romanization

चाय → chai, not cāy. पानी → paani, not pānī. We romanize the way Indians actually type.

Each entry shows synonyms, broader terms, and narrower terms — built from WordNet's 40,000+ synset hierarchy.
